Arepas

Arepa is a type of food made of ground maize dough, eaten in the northern region of South America since pre-Columbian times, and notable primarily in the cuisine of Bolivia and other countries.

It is commonly eaten in those countries and can be served with accompaniments such as cheese, cuajada, various meats, chicken, avocado, or 'diablito' (deviled ham spread). It can also be split tomake sandwiches.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups warm water
  • 2 cups pre-cooked cornmeal
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter, or as needed

Steps

  1. Combine water, cornmeal, and salt in a bowl. Mix together and let sit until water is absorbed, about 5 minutes. Form dough into patties about 5 inches across and 1/2-inch thick.
  2. Coat a griddle with butter. Cook patties, one at a time, over medium-high heat until lightly browned, about 3 minutes per side.
